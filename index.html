About Me

My name is Xiao Lin Guan, and my life is a testament to the power of determination and personal growth. At the age of 12, I embarked on a life-changing journey from China to a new land. The challenges were numerous, primarily centered around the language barrier. However, I quickly realized that mastering English was essential for my personal and professional development. I took it upon myself to bridge this gap, starting by reading articles aloud in English and seeking guidance from my family. Gradually, my pronunciation improved, and so did my self-confidence. Through persistent effort and engaging in English conversations, I found my voice in this new environment.

In 2022, I proudly graduated with a degree in computer science, but my thirst for knowledge and growth drove me to enroll in App Academy in May 2023. This decision reflects my unwavering commitment to enhancing my coding skills and gaining practical experience before entering the working world. Beyond coding, you'll often find me engrossed in a good book, refining my badminton skills on the court, or, I confess, indulging in karaoke, even if my singing occasionally tests the limits of my friends' tolerance! My strengths lie in my flexibility, diligence, and a positive mindset, all of which have shaped me into who I am today. As I look ahead, I envision myself thriving as a software engineer, contributing my skills to projects that create a real impact. I invite you to join me on this journey of discovery and growth, and together, let's connect, collaborate, and make a positive change.

JMAX

A problem solver web app that allows users to post problems they meet in daily life and look for help from other users. They can create requests under three categories, Home Repair, Delivery and Driver. Other users can respond to the requests by providing offers to the requesters. Users can chat with each other privately to have a conversation online and requesters can accept the offer and add comments to the offers which act like reviews.

Yep!

A web app clone of Yelp that allows users to view restaurants with exact locations on google map. Users can search for restaurants they are interested in and displays basic info of the specific restaurant they click on. Users can filter the restaurants based on price range, zip code and ratings. They can also post reviews and ratings on the restaurants to help others choose a restaurant.

Hey Wake Up!

A browser game built with JavaScript that challenges the users' clicking speed on dog images within a limited time duration. This game took inspiration from daily lectures where students trying hard to stay awake when the instructors are not looking at them. Users are expected to keep clicking on each dog image to make sure their energy level doesn't drop below 50% when the timer stops. When it's below 50%, it means the dogs are falling asleep while above 50% means the dogs are energetic.
